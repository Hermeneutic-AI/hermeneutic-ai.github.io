Hermeneutic.AI is a social science research lab devoted to **interpretive artificial intelligence**.

We start from a simple observation: the interpretive disciplines — sociology, anthropology, history, and their neighbors in the humanities — have been largely spectators to the current wave of artificial intelligence, which speaks the language of prediction and benchmark. Yet the systems at the center of that wave are trained on text, culture, and conversation: the very materials interpretive scholars have spent more than a century learning to read well.

The lab works in both directions across that divide:

- **Studying AI interpretively.** Treating language models as objects of hermeneutic and social-scientific inquiry — asking what kind of readers they are, what pre-understandings they carry, and how they participate in the circulation of meaning.
- **Building AI for interpretive scholarship.** Designing instruments — like our [Journal Monitor and Newsfeed Monitor](/projects/) — that extend the reach of close reading without replacing the judgment at its core.

Our [research notes](/notes/) are written in the open and in a provisional spirit. We welcome conversation with scholars, students, and builders who share these questions — see the [contact page](/contact/).
